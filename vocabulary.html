Massage & Medical Terminology Breakdown

Here is a comprehensive vocabulary list focusing on massage-related and medical terminology, with an emphasis on how to break down words into their component parts (prefixes, root words, and suffixes). Understanding these building blocks will significantly help you decipher new terms encountered in your MBLEx preparation and professional practice.

Understanding Medical Terminology: Prefixes, Roots, and Suffixes

Medical and anatomical terms are often constructed from Greek and Latin word parts. By learning common prefixes (beginning), root words (core meaning), and suffixes (ending), you can often deduce the meaning of complex terms even if you haven't seen them before.

Full Medical & Massage-Related Terms Breakdown

Here are some terms relevant to massage therapy, broken down to help with understanding:

  • Myofascial:
    • Myo-: muscle
    • Fascial: fascia (connective tissue)
    • Meaning: Pertaining to muscle and fascia.
  • Contraindication:
    • Contra-: against
    • Indication: a reason to use a treatment
    • Meaning: A condition or factor that serves as a reason to withhold a certain medical treatment due to the harm that it would cause the patient.
  • Effleurage:
    • (French origin, not easily broken into standard prefixes/roots/suffixes)
    • Meaning: A light, gliding massage stroke used for warming tissue, spreading lubricant, and relaxation.
  • Petrissage:
    • (French origin)
    • Meaning: Kneading massage strokes that lift, roll, and squeeze tissue.
  • Tapotement:
    • (French origin)
    • Meaning: Percussive massage strokes like tapping, hacking, or cupping.
  • Proprioception:
    • Proprio-: one's own
    • -ception: sensing
    • Meaning: The sense of the relative position of one's own body parts and strength of effort being used in movement.
  • Lymphedema:
    • Lymph-: lymphatic system
    • -edema: swelling
    • Meaning: Swelling due to an accumulation of lymph fluid.
  • Dermatitis:
    • Derm-: skin
    • -itis: inflammation
    • Meaning: Inflammation of the skin.
  • Neuropathy:
    • Neuro-: nerve
    • -pathy: disease
    • Meaning: Disease or dysfunction of one or more peripheral nerves.
  • Hypertension:
    • Hyper-: excessive
    • Tension: pressure
    • Meaning: Abnormally high blood pressure.
  • Hypoglycemia:
    • Hypo-: deficient
    • Glyc-: sugar
    • -emia: blood condition
    • Meaning: Abnormally low blood sugar.
  • Osteoporosis:
    • Osteo-: bone
    • Por-: porous
    • -osis: condition
    • Meaning: A condition in which bones become weak and brittle.
  • Fibromyalgia:
    • Fibr-: fibrous tissue
    • Myo-: muscle
    • -algia: pain
    • Meaning: A chronic disorder characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and tenderness in localized areas.
  • Sprain:
    • (English origin)
    • Meaning: A stretching or tearing of ligaments (the tough bands of fibrous tissue that connect two bones together in your joints).
  • Strain:
    • (English origin)
    • Meaning: A stretching or tearing of muscle or tendon.
  • Hematoma:
    • Hemat-: blood
    • -oma: mass/collection
    • Meaning: A solid swelling of clotted blood within the tissues (a bruise).
  • Edema:
    • (Greek origin)
    • Meaning: Swelling caused by excess fluid trapped in your body's tissues.

Conclusion and Suggestions for Study:

This list provides a strong foundation for breaking down and understanding complex medical and massage terms. By consistently applying the prefix-root-suffix method, you'll find that many intimidating words become much more manageable.

Suggestions for further study:

  • Practice Daily: Pick a few terms each day and try to break them down before looking up their definitions.
  • Create Flashcards: Write the full term on one side and its breakdown, meaning, and a relevant example on the other.
  • Contextualize: When you encounter a new term in your textbooks or practice, try to relate it to the body system or condition it describes.
  • Draw Diagrams: For anatomical terms, sketching the structure can help solidify your understanding.
  • Use Your Quiz App: As you go through the questions in your MBLEx prep app, pay close attention to the terminology used in the questions, options, and explanations, and try to break down unfamiliar words.

Understanding this vocabulary is not just for the exam; it's fundamental to becoming a competent and confident massage therapist who can communicate effectively with clients and other healthcare professionals.
